17 September 1950

Who is Narendra Damodar Das Modi

Narendra Damodardas Modi is the 14th and current Prime Minister of India, in office since 26 May 2014. He was the Chief Minister of Gujarat from 2001 to 2014 and is the Member of Parliament for Varanasi. He is a member of the Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP) and of the Rashtriya Swayamsevak Sangh (RSS), a right-wing Hindu nationalist paramilitary volunteer organisation. He is the longest-serving prime minister from outside the Indian National Congress.

Achievement

Narendra Modi has achieved a number of things during his time as Prime Minister of India, including:

  • Economic growth: India's economy has grown at an average rate of 7% per year under Modi's leadership. This has helped to lift millions of people out of poverty.
  • Infrastructure development: Modi has invested heavily in infrastructure development, including roads, railways, and airports. This has made it easier for businesses to operate and has improved the quality of life for many Indians.
  • Social welfare programs: Modi has implemented a number of social welfare programs, such as the Swachh Bharat Mission (Clean India Mission) and the Pradhan Mantri Jan Dhan Yojana (Prime Minister's People's Wealth Scheme). These programs have helped to improve the lives of millions of poor and marginalized Indians.
  • Foreign policy: Modi has pursued a more assertive foreign policy than his predecessors. He has strengthened ties with the United States and other Western countries, while also maintaining close relations with Russia and China.

Some of Modi's other specific achievements include:

  • GST: Modi's government introduced the Goods and Services Tax (GST) in 2017. This is India's biggest tax reform since independence and has helped to simplify the tax system and boost economic growth.
  • Jan Dhan Yojana: The Pradhan Mantri Jan Dhan Yojana (Prime Minister's People's Wealth Scheme) was launched in 2014 to provide bank accounts to all Indians. This has helped to bring millions of people into the formal financial system.
  • Swachh Bharat Mission: The Swachh Bharat Mission (Clean India Mission) was launched in 2014 to improve sanitation and hygiene in India. The mission has helped to build millions of toilets and has made India a cleaner country.
  • Ayushman Bharat: The Ayushman Bharat Yojana (Pradhan Mantri Jan Arogya Yojana) is a national health insurance scheme that was launched in 2018. The scheme provides coverage to over 500 million Indians and is one of the largest healthcare programs in the world.
